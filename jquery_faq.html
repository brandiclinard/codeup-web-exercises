National Park Strange Facts!

Vermont
The Rockefeller family built a nuclear fallout shelter under the bowling alley at Marsh-Billings-Rockefeller National Historical Park in Vermont.
South Dakota
A South Dakota miner started calling Mount Rushmore by this name after a visiting New York attorney – Charles Rushmore – inquired about the name of the granite cliff. The name stuck and became official in 1930.
California
The NPS oversees two former World War II Japanese internment camp sites: Manzanar and Minidoka national historic sites.
Nevada/Utah
Bristlecone pine trees, found in western national parks such as Great Basin National Park in Nevada and Bryce Canyon National Park in Utah can be up to 5,000 years old.
UTAH
Zion National Park has a popular slot canyon whose tubular appearance earns it the moniker, “the Subway.”
Wyoming
According to Kiowa Native American legend, Devils Tower National Monument was created when seven young girls chased by bears jumped on a low rock and prayed for help. The rock rose, lifting the girls out of reach. The bears then scratched deep gouges in the enormous pillar of rock.
Florida
Everglades National Park is the only place in the world where American alligators and American crocodiles co-exist.
Arizona
Many trees in Arizona’s Petrified Forest National Park are about 218 million years old. Though they died long ago and are actually tree fossils.
Hawaii
Kalaupapa National Historical Park in Hawaii was first established in 1866 as a leper colony. A few former patients, who are no longer contagious, still live there today.
Colorado
Mesa Verde National Park includes ruins of 600 cliff dwellings and is the largest archaeological preserve in the U.S.
